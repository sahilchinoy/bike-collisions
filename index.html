Bicycle Accidents in Berkeley

SAHIL CHINOY · January 2015

This is a work in progress being developed for the Daily Californian.

Notes

  • 83.8% of collisions occurred in clear conditions, 12.3% in cloudy conditions and 3.1% in rain or fog
  • 2009 was the worst year on record, with 203 accidents; in 2013 there were 160
  • September and October are the most dangerous months, with more than 20% of collisions occuring during these two months alone
  • The hours between 4 and 7 pm were especially dangerous for bicyclists; the 5 pm hour accounted for more than 10% of accidents

Sources and methodology

Data is from the California Highway Patrol's Statewide Integrated Traffic Records System, which aggregates data collected at the collision scene. Their records include a field that indicates whether a bicycle was involved in each collision. In the Berkeley Police Department's jurisdiction, a total of 2,227 incidents since January 2001 have involved a bicycle.

The data also indicates the severity of the accident, on a scale from 0 to 4. Fatal accidents are assigned a 1, severe injuries are assigned a 2, and so forth. In calculating the danger index, incidents with a severity of 1 added 10 points to the index, incidents with a 2 added 5 points, incidents with a 3 added 3 points, incidents with a 4 added 2 points, and incidents with a 0 added 1 point. Each intersection was normalized against the maximum total of 45 points at Dwight Way and Milvia Street.
